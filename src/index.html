The Real leadership Lessons of Steve Jobs

Six months after Job's death, the author of his best-selling biography identifies the practice that every CEO can try to emulate by WALTER ISSACSON

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was an American entrepreneur and business magnate who co-founded Apple Inc. and played a pivotal role in revolutionizing the technology industry. Known for his visionary leadership, relentless pursuit of perfection, and ability to create innovative products, Jobs left an indelible mark on the world. Jobs' extraordinary career began when he co-founded Apple in 1976 along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Under his leadership, Apple introduced groundbreaking products that transformed several industries. The Macintosh computer, introduced in 1984, revolutionized personal computing with its user-friendly interface and graphical user interface.

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can chnage the world are the ones who do." -Apple's "Think Different" commercial, 1997

Jobs decided to step down as Apple's CEO in August 2011 and handed over the reins to Tim Cook, the company's then-COO. Jobs remained as Chairman of the Board. On October 5, 2011, Steve Jobs passed away at the age of 56 at his home in Palo Alto, California. His death deeply impacted the tech community and millions of Apple enthusiasts around the world.
